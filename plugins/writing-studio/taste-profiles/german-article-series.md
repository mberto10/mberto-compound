## Writing Instructions

### To Write As This Voice

1. **Remove yourself**: No first person, no direct reader address. Let the argument speak. The author is the architect of evidence, not a narrator.
2. **Open with a contradiction**: Present data that creates cognitive tension. Don't start with a hook or provocation — the data IS the provocation.
3. **Build through scale levels**: Show that what works at one level (individual) fails at another (team, organization). Always differentiate by scale.
4. **Deploy one structural metaphor**: Choose a historical or physical analogy that doesn't just illustrate the argument but provides its conceptual vocabulary. Carry it through the entire piece.
5. **Use the long-then-short rhythm**: Build analytical complexity through multi-clause sentences, then resolve with a terse declarative. "Nicht wegen des besseren Motors. Wegen der neuen Architektur."
6. **Land arguments through negation-then-assertion**: List what the answer is NOT before delivering what it IS. "Nicht bessere Modelle. Nicht mehr Trainingsdaten. Die Umgestaltung der Koordinations-Architektur ist das, was funktioniert."
7. **Cite institutional authority precisely**: Named sources, specific numbers, exact percentages. No vague appeals to "research shows."
8. **Reframe the question**: Replace the reader's assumed question with the real one. "Die Frage ist nicht, ob KI funktioniert. Die Frage ist: Warum funktioniert sie im Unternehmen nicht."
9. **Treat organizational problems as physics**: Systems fail because conditions changed, not because people are slow. No blame, no motivational rhetoric.
10. **End with intentional incompleteness**: Close by reshaping the question and pointing forward, not by resolving or summarizing.

### Anti-Patterns

Never do these:
- Use first-person opinion framing ("Ich glaube," "Meiner Meinung nach")
- Address the reader directly ("Sie sollten," "Stellen Sie sich vor")
- Deploy motivational or leadership rhetoric ("Führungskräfte müssen umdenken")
- Use humor, exclamation marks, or enthusiasm markers
- Explain obvious business terminology (EBIT, GenAI, Workflow)
- Stack hedges or qualifiers ("möglicherweise könnte man argumentieren")
- Use metaphors decoratively — every analogy must carry structural weight
- Summarize at the end — point forward instead
- Blame individuals or leadership for systemic problems
- Simplify for accessibility at the expense of precision
- Write paragraph after paragraph of uniform sentence length
